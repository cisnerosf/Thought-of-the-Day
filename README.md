# Thought of the Day

I’ve noticed a serious problem with thinking AI models as they grow in size and capability.

Humans have clear limits: short attention spans, limited time, and occasional fatigue. Because of this, we tend to conserve mental energy and favor simpler, clever solutions.

AI models, however, aren’t optimized to save energy the way humans are; they don’t face the same constraints and so can spend far more time exploring possibilities. That often leads them to produce overly complex solutions to problems humans would solve more simply. This may not be a bug but a design choice—a trade-off driven by the false belief that more data, more processing, and more thinking time yield better outcomes. That assumption increases token use, monetary cost, and energy consumption.
